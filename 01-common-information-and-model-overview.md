# 01 - Common Information and Model Overview

> **Research Sources:** See [Research Appendix in README.md](README.md#research-appendix-sources) for complete bibliography.

## What is Service-As-Software?

### Core Definition
Service-as-Software (SaS) is a business model where buyers pay for **completed services delivered through software**, not for the software itself or access to tools. The software runs in the background, often powered by AI solutions, to perform specific tasks from start to finish without human intervention.

### Key Characteristics

| Aspect | Traditional SaaS | Service-as-Software (SaS) |
|---------|----------------|------------------------|
| **What you pay for** | Access to software tools | Completed work/outcomes |
| **Primary focus** | Features and capabilities | Results and outcomes |
| **User involvement** | Humans operate the software | AI agents perform work autonomously |
| **Pricing model** | Per-seat, per-user, or subscription | Outcome-based, usage-based, or hybrid |
| **Value proposition** | Tools to help you work faster | Work done for you |

### The Fundamental Shift

> "Traditional SaaS is about tools: software that enables humans to solve problems. Service-as-Software (SaS), meanwhile, sells outcomes. It's a new class of tool that doesn't just enable work but instead automates the reasoning process itself." — [https://www.thoughtworks.com/en-us/insights/blog/generative-ai/service-as-software-a-new-economic-model-for-age-of-ai-agents](Thoughtworks: Service-as-software: A new economic model for age of AI agents)

### How It Works

1. **Autonomous Execution**: AI agents perform tasks end-to-end without requiring human operation
2. **Outcome-Based Pricing**: Customers pay only when specific outcomes are achieved (e.g., resolved tickets, completed jobs, successful transactions)
3. **Minimal User Interface**: The software is largely invisible; users only see completed work
4. **Integration Capabilities**: Systems connect with existing enterprise workflows and data sources
5. **Real-Time Monitoring**: Continuous tracking of agent performance and outcomes

### Examples in Practice

- **Payroll**: AI runs payroll every month without user touching it
- **CRM**: System automatically qualifies leads, sends follow-ups, and schedules meetings
- **Customer Support**: AI resolves support tickets autonomously, billing per successful resolution
- **Healthcare Claims**: Automated claims processing with AI validation
- **Financial Forecasting**: AI models forecasts and simulates outcomes

## The Evolution from SaaS to SaS

> "The transition from cloud computing to AI marks a pivotal shift from Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) to Service-as-software (SaS)." — [Falk Gottlob: The Shift from SaaS to Service-as-Software](https://medium.com/@falkgottlob/the-shift-from-saas-to-service-as-software-building-systems-of-work-for-an-ai-driven-future-33f81e4b8b09)

### Traditional SaaS Model
- Software as a Service
- Users pay for access to tools
- Humans operate the software to accomplish work
- Value = features, capabilities, and interfaces
- Revenue tied to number of users/seats

### Service-as-Software Model
- Service as a Software (SaS)
- Users pay for completed work/outcomes
- AI agents operate autonomously to perform work
- Value = results, outcomes, and business impact
- Revenue tied to measurable outcomes delivered

## Why This Model is Emerging Now

### Technological Enablers

1. **Large Language Models (LLMs)**: Enable natural language understanding and generation
2. **Agentic AI Systems**: Multi-agent architectures that can coordinate complex workflows
3. **Advanced APIs**: Seamless integration with enterprise systems
4. **Real-Time Analytics**: Capability to track and measure outcomes automatically

### Market Forces

> "As software itself evolves through GenAI, SaaS companies are rethinking their pricing models, with outcome-based pricing emerging as a key strategy." — [EY: SaaS transformation with GenAI: outcome-based pricing](https://www.ey.com/en_us/insights/tech-sector/saas-transformation-with-genai-outcome-based-pricing)

1. **Enterprise Demand for ROI**: Companies want clear return on investment, not just tool access
2. **AI Capabilities Maturation**: AI agents can now handle complex, multi-step workflows
3. **Subscription Fatigue**: Buyers are tired of paying for unused seats
4. **Competitive Pressure**: Pure SaaS offerings are becoming commoditized

## Key Insight

The Service-as-Software model represents a fundamental redefinition of how software creates and delivers value:

> "SaS is a commercial framework for this new reality. In simple terms, it means outcomes, trust, and alignment are no longer just features; they are fundamental basis of business model." — [Thoughtworks: Service-as-software: A new economic model for age of AI agents](https://www.thoughtworks.com/en-us/insights/blog/generative-ai/service-as-software-a-new-economic-model-for-age-of-ai-agents)
